## Sequencing Rationale

1. ต้องควบคุมปวด + drainage ก่อน — ลด bacterial load
2. RCT ก่อน crown — restoration permanent ต้องวางบน foundation ที่ pulp ไม่มีโรค
3. Cuspal coverage ภายใน 3 เดือน — endo-treated molars มี fracture risk สูง (Aquilino & Caplan 2002)

## ⚠️ Risks + Complications

### Endo
- Procedural: file separation, ledge formation, perforation, sodium hypochlorite accident
- Post-op: flare-up (5-15%), persistent pain, need for retreat (5-10%)
- Long-term: vertical root fracture (esp. without cuspal coverage)

### Crown
- Sensitivity post-cementation
- Marginal leakage → 2° caries
- Failure: ~5% per year (zirconia better than PFM long-term)

## 💊 Materia Medica — สำหรับ case นี้

### Local Anesthetic
- **Lidocaine 2% with epi 1:80,000**
- IAN block + long buccal infiltration
- Max dose 7 mg/kg, max 500 mg
- Onset 3-5 min, duration 1-2 hr

### Endo materials
- **Irrigation:** NaOCl 5.25% (5-10 ml/canal)
  - Caveat: avoid extrusion → "hypochlorite accident" (severe pain, swelling)
- **Final irrigation:** EDTA 17% (smear layer removal) → saline
- **Sealer:** AH Plus (epoxy-based, gold standard)
- **Obturation:** Gutta-percha + warm vertical condensation

### Restorative
- **Temporary:** IRM or Cavit
- **Core build-up:** dual-cure composite
- **Crown:** zirconia preferred for posterior (long-term fracture resistance)
