## 🔍 DDx — Feline Chronic Vomiting

### 📍 Common (must consider — เริ่มจากนี้ก่อน)

#### 1. **Chronic enteropathy / IBD (Inflammatory Bowel Disease)**
- **Supporting:** chronic intermittent vomiting, weight loss, may have soft stools
- **Diagnostic:**
  - CBC, chem, T4, fPLI, cobalamin/folate
  - Abdominal U/S (intestinal wall thickening, mesenteric LN)
  - Definitive: GI biopsy (endoscopic or surgical)
- **Treatment:** dietary trial (hydrolyzed/novel protein) → immunosuppression (prednisolone) ± metronidazole
- **Prognosis:** chronic management, often lifelong

#### 2. **Hyperthyroidism** (very common in cat ≥ 8 yrs)
- **Supporting:** weight loss + polyphagia, vomiting, tachycardia, palpable goiter
- **Diagnostic:** **T4** (must do in any cat ≥ 7 with weight loss/vomiting)
- **Treatment:** methimazole, I-131, surgery, diet (Y/D)
- **Pearl:** ALWAYS check T4 before attributing chronic vomiting to GI disease in older cats

#### 3. **Chronic kidney disease (CKD)** (also very common in older cats)
- **Supporting:** PU/PD, weight loss, vomiting (uremic), poor coat
- **Diagnostic:** BUN, creatinine, **SDMA**, U/A (USG), BP
- **IRIS staging** (1-4)
- **Treatment:** renal diet, fluids (SC at home if Stage 3+), phosphate binder, K supplementation, antiemetic, ACEi (if proteinuric)

#### 4. **Dietary indiscretion / food allergy**
- **Supporting:** vomits within hours of eating, GI signs
- **Diagnostic:** elimination diet trial 8-12 weeks (hydrolyzed protein)
- **Treatment:** novel protein or hydrolyzed diet long-term

#### 5. **Trichobezoar (hairball)** — but in 9 yo with weight loss, less likely sole diagnosis
- **Supporting:** intermittent regurgitation, hairballs visible
- **Treatment:** brushing, hairball remedy, fiber

### 📍 Less common

#### 6. **Pancreatitis (chronic)**
- **Diagnostic:** **fPLI** (Spec-fPL) > 5.4 μg/L abnormal
- **Treatment:** supportive (fluids, antiemetics, analgesia, B12)

#### 7. **GI lymphoma** (esp. in older cats)
- **Supporting:** progressive weight loss + vomiting, mass on U/S
- **Diagnostic:** GI biopsy + immunohistochemistry / PARR
- **Treatment:** chemotherapy (chlorambucil + prednisolone for small cell)

### 📍 Rare but emergencies (rule out)

#### 8. **GI obstruction (linear foreign body — string, thread)**
- **Cat-specific risk:** thread/string under tongue
- **MUST exam under tongue** in every vomiting cat
- **Treatment:** emergency exploratory + foreign body removal

#### 9. **Megaesophagus / esophageal foreign body**
- **Diagnostic:** thoracic radiograph + barium

## 💡 Approach (Recommended Diagnostic Plan)

### Day 1 (initial visit):
- **History:** diet, treats, indoor/outdoor, household stressors, hairball frequency
- **PE:** **MUST examine under tongue** for linear FB, palpate thyroid, abdominal palpation
- **Minimum database:**
  - CBC + chemistry + T4
  - U/A with USG
  - **fPLI (Spec-fPL)**
  - **Cobalamin + folate**
  - Fecal direct + flotation (rule out parasites)

### Day 7 (results review):
- ถ้า T4 ↑ → hyperthyroidism workup
- ถ้า cobalamin ↓ → supplement + investigate GI
- ถ้า fPLI ↑ → pancreatitis treatment
- ถ้า all WNL → abdominal U/S

### Week 2-4:
- ถ้า U/S equivocal → empirical dietary trial 4-8 weeks
- ถ้า no response → endoscopic biopsy

## ⚠️ Species-Specific Warnings (Feline)

### 🚫 **NEVER use in cats**
- **Paracetamol/acetaminophen** — LETHAL (methemoglobinemia, hepatic necrosis); cats lack glucuronidation
- **Permethrin** (in dog flea products) — neurotoxic, fatal
- **Lily** (Lilium, Hemerocallis) — even pollen lick = AKI
- **NSAIDs** — extreme caution; meloxicam approved at 0.05 mg/kg PO q24h (chronic) only after fluid therapy + normal renal/hydration; NEVER in CKD/dehydrated/hypovolemic cats

### ⚠️ Use with caution
- **Tramadol** — bitter taste (cats hate); mu-opioid agonist with serotonergic activity; CRI is better tolerated
- **Buprenorphine** — preferred opioid in cats (sublingual works due to alkaline saliva)

## 🚩 Red Flags — refer / รีบ

- Hypovolemic shock — IV fluid + ER
- Suspect linear foreign body (string under tongue) — surgical emergency
- Severe dehydration + anuria → AKI → may need IV fluid + dialysis
- Severe hypokalemia (< 3.0) — cardiac risk → KCl supplementation IV
- Hypertensive crisis (in CKD/hyperthyroid) → amlodipine

## ⚠️ Caveats

- This is reference template — every case different
- T4 may be normal in early hyperthyroidism (test fT4 by ED, or T3 suppression)
- Maropitant labeled for 5 days SC — extended use is off-label (some clinicians use longer with monitoring)
- Owner counseling: medication compliance, recheck schedule, what signs require ER
- ไม่ทดแทน clinical judgment
